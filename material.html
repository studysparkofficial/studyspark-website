🌡️ Thermodynamics: System and Its Types

🔬 Introduction

Thermodynamics is a branch of chemistry that studies energy changes — especially the transfer of energy in the form of heat and work. It helps us understand how and why certain processes happen, and how energy flows within systems.

🌍 System and the Surroundings

System: A specific region or collection of matter where a thermodynamic process occurs.

Surroundings: Everything outside the system — the rest of the universe that can interact with the system.

Boundary: A real or imaginary line that separates the system from the surroundings. It can be fixed or movable.

  • Rigid Boundary: Doesn’t change shape or volume (e.g., a closed, rigid container).
  • Diathermic Boundary: Allows heat transfer (e.g., metal lid of a beaker).
  • Adiabatic Boundary: No heat transfer allowed (e.g., a perfectly insulated container).

📦 Types of System

1. Open System: Can exchange both matter and energy with its surroundings.
Example: A boiling pot of water – heat and steam escape.

2. Closed System: Can exchange energy (heat or work), but not matter.
Example: A sealed jar of gas.

3. Isolated System: Can exchange neither matter nor energy.
Example: An insulated thermos flask.

Khoobsurat Baat 💡
The universe is a perfect example of an isolated system. 🌌
Since there is nothing beyond it, no heat or mass can go out or come in.
